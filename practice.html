7-Day Practice Plan

10–20 minutes a day to integrate “You are awareness, not the story of a self.”

Day 1: Thought labeling and pausing

  • Practice (10x): When a thought arises, pause 5–10 seconds and label it: “planning,” “worry,” “judgment,” “memory.”
  • Notice tone and body sensations. In the evening, journal one paragraph on what changed when you paused.

Day 2: Emotion as weather

  • When a strong emotion appears, widen attention to breath, posture, and peripheral sounds. Label the emotion and 2 body sensations.
  • Take one small, values-aligned step regardless of the story (e.g., send the email, take a walk). Journal the effect.

Day 3: Condition engineering

  • Pick one unhelpful loop (e.g., doomscrolling). Change one condition: move the phone, set app limits, change lighting.
  • Replace with a 5-minute grounding habit (stretching, breath, tidy). Journal before/after state.

Day 4: Context shifts identity

  • Notice how your “self” shifts across contexts (home/work/alone). Jot 3 differences.
  • Intentionally change one variable (music, workspace, posture) and observe mood/behavior changes. Journal which had most leverage.

Day 5: Values over narratives

  • Write your top 3 values (e.g., honesty, learning, care).
  • For each decision, ask: “What’s the smallest action that expresses a value now?” Journal one case where this replaced rumination.

Day 6: Non-doership micro-practice

  • For 5 minutes, let breath and sounds occur without management. Notice thoughts arise and pass without a central controller.
  • In one conversation, relax control over wording; let speech “play through” while staying kind/clear. Journal clarity/ease.

Day 7: Integration and compassion

  • 10-minute open awareness: include breath, body, sounds, thoughts, and space simultaneously.
  • When encountering another’s reactivity, silently note “weather in awareness,” and respond with one supportive behavior.
  • Review the week’s highest-leverage condition changes and set one commitment for next week.

Optional daily anchors

  • Morning (3 min): “What’s present? What one small values-aligned action now?”
  • Evening (3 min): “Where did I identify with a story? What condition can I tweak tomorrow?”
